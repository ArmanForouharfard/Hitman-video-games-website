Subject Profile

The Perfect
Assassin

Born of genetic engineering and trained to be the ultimate weapon, Agent 47 is the most accomplished contract killer in the world. Identified only by a barcode tattooed on the back of his head, he is the product of decades of classified experimentation conducted by the elusive Dr. Ort-Meyer.

Calm, methodical, and utterly remorseless, 47 operates through the International Contract Agency — a shadowy organisation that sells precision lethality to the highest bidder. Every target is a puzzle. Every location a stage. Every kill, a masterpiece of planning.

Since his debut in 2000, Agent 47 has become one of gaming's most iconic characters — a figure who blends cold efficiency with an almost theatrical sense of style.

A History of
Precision

From the alleys of Hong Kong to the skyscrapers of Dubai

2000
2000 · IO Interactive / Eidos
Hitman: Codename 47
The debut that launched a legend. Set across Hong Kong, Colombia, and Budapest, this gritty third-person action game introduced a genetically engineered killer who escapes his laboratory prison to hunt down his creators. Its non-linear level design and disguise mechanics were revolutionary for the era, planting the seeds of the entire sandbox assassination genre.
PC
2002
2002 · IO Interactive
Hitman 2: Silent Assassin
The game that defined the franchise's identity. Introducing the beloved "Silent Assassin" rating system that rewarded patience over brute force, this entry refined the disguise and stealth mechanics substantially. Spanning Sicily, India, Japan and St. Petersburg, it remains one of the most beloved in the series — balancing atmosphere, tension, and strategic creativity.
PC · PS2 · Xbox · GameCube
2004
2004 · IO Interactive
Hitman: Contracts
A darker, more atmospheric chapter structured as fever-dream flashbacks from a wounded 47. Revisiting key moments from Codename 47 with rebuilt visuals, Contracts established a bleaker, more cinematic tone — rain-soaked European locations, brutal kills, and an almost gothic sensibility that set it apart from its predecessors.
PC · PS2 · Xbox
2006
2006 · IO Interactive
Hitman: Blood Money
Widely regarded as the masterpiece of the classic era. Blood Money brought notoriety systems, weapon upgrades, and iconic sandbox missions including the infamous opera house assassination, a White House infiltration, and a Mardi Gras parade hit. Its perfect balance of comedy and menace, along with the legendary closing sequence to "Ave Maria," cemented its status as a landmark in gaming.
PC · PS2 · Xbox · Xbox 360
2012
2012 · IO Interactive
Hitman: Absolution
A divisive but technically impressive chapter that prioritised story over pure sandbox play. 47 goes rogue to protect a young girl, leading to a more personal, cinematic narrative. While it divided the fanbase by streamlining the classic formula, Absolution pushed graphical fidelity and set a new bar for character animation, paving the way for IO's reinvention.
PC · PS3 · Xbox 360
2016
2016–2021 · IO Interactive
The World of Assassination Trilogy
IO Interactive's triumphant return to form — and a complete reinvention of the franchise. Released across three chapters between 2016 and 2021, the trilogy introduced massive, densely layered sandbox environments: Paris, Sapienza, Marrakesh, Bangkok, Miami, Santa Fortuna, Mumbai, Dartmoor, Berlin and Dubai. Live content, escalation contracts, and Elusive Targets created a perpetually evolving world. The franchise sold over 50 million copies total. HITMAN: World of Assassination, the consolidated experience, remains the definitive edition of the series.
